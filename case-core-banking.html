Core Banking Account Lifecycle Optimization

Role: Product Manager
Domain: Core Banking / Retail Deposits

1. Context & Background

In many mid-size banks, customer account lifecycle management (opening, maintenance, dormancy, and closure) is supported by legacy core banking systems such as Temenos T24.

While these systems are functionally rich, customer experience and operational efficiency often suffer due to fragmented workflows, manual interventions, and inconsistent handling across channels.

2. Problem Statement

  • Account opening took 2–3 working days
  • High dependency on branch-level manual verification
  • Dormant and inactive accounts created compliance risk
  • Customer requests (updates, closure) lacked transparency
Core PM Question: How can we streamline the full account lifecycle while respecting core banking constraints and regulatory requirements?

3. Users & Stakeholders

  • Primary: Retail banking customers
  • Secondary: Branch operations staff
  • Stakeholders: Compliance, IT, Audit teams

4. Goals & Success Metrics

North Star Metric: Account Activation Time

Supporting Metrics

  • First successful transaction rate
  • Account opening completion rate
  • Customer service tickets per account

Guardrail Metrics

  • KYC exceptions
  • Audit observations
  • Dormant account growth

5. Discovery & Key Insights

  • Customers cared more about predictability than speed alone
  • Operations teams lacked a unified view of account status
  • Compliance teams required traceability at every stage
Insight that shaped the product: Lifecycle visibility was more valuable than adding new features.

6. Solution Design

Designed an Account Lifecycle Orchestration Layer on top of the core banking system. 

Customer Request
   ↓
Lifecycle Status Engine
   ↓
Validation (KYC / Rules)
   ↓
Core Banking (T24)
   ↓
Notifications & Audit Logs

Key Capabilities

  • Unified lifecycle status tracking
  • Rule-driven validations
  • Event-based notifications
  • Audit-ready logs

7. Product Requirements (PRD – Summary)

Objective

Reduce account activation time while ensuring compliance and operational consistency.

In Scope

  • Savings & current accounts
  • KYC status tracking
  • Dormancy and reactivation flows
  • Customer-initiated updates

Out of Scope

  • Replacement of core banking system
  • Credit products

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Reliability
  • Auditability
  • Backward compatibility

8. MVP Scope & Roadmap

Phase 1 (MVP)

  • Account opening visibility
  • Lifecycle status dashboard

Phase 2

  • Dormancy alerts
  • Self-service account updates

Explicit Non-Goals

  • Real-time core replacement
  • Cross-product consolidation

9. Analytics & Measurement

Tracked Events

  • Account created
  • KYC completed
  • Account activated
  • Account dormant

Decision Triggers

  • Activation > 48 hrs → Process review
  • Dormant growth → Customer nudges

10. Outcome & Learnings

  • Faster and more predictable account activation
  • Reduced operational escalations
  • Improved audit readiness

11. Key Learnings

  • Visibility beats raw speed in regulated products
  • Lifecycle thinking prevents downstream issues
