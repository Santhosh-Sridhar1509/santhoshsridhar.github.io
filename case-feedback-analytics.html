Customer Feedback Analytics Platform

Role: Product Manager
Domain: Customer Experience / Analytics

1. Context & Background

Banks receive customer feedback through multiple channels: support tickets, surveys, emails, app reviews, and branch complaints.

This data was fragmented, manually analyzed, and rarely used for proactive product decisions.

2. Problem Statement

  • Feedback analysis was reactive and delayed
  • No single view of customer sentiment
  • Product teams relied on anecdotes, not data
  • High-effort manual tagging
Core PM Question: How can we convert raw customer feedback into actionable insights for product and operations teams?

3. Users & Stakeholders

  • Primary: Product managers
  • Secondary: CX and operations teams
  • Stakeholders: Leadership, Compliance

4. Goals & Success Metrics

North Star Metric: Actioned Insights per Month

Supporting Metrics

  • Feedback processing time
  • Insight-to-action conversion rate
  • Repeat issue reduction

Guardrail Metrics

  • False-positive insights
  • Model explainability

5. Discovery & Key Insights

  • Most feedback clusters around a small set of recurring issues
  • PMs wanted trends, not individual complaints
  • Explainability was critical for trust in AI-generated insights
Insight that shaped the product: Summarization and clustering mattered more than sentiment scores alone.

6. Solution Design

Designed an AI-assisted analytics platform that clusters feedback, summarizes themes, and highlights trends. 

Feedback Sources
   ↓
Data Normalization
   ↓
AI Clustering & Summarization
   ↓
Insights Dashboard
   ↓
Product Decisions

Key Capabilities

  • Automated topic clustering
  • Explainable summaries
  • Trend detection over time
  • Insight export for PRDs

7. Product Requirements (PRD – Summary)

Objective

Enable PMs to make data-backed decisions using customer feedback at scale.

In Scope

  • Text-based feedback sources
  • AI-based clustering and summarization
  • Insight dashboards

Out of Scope

  • Voice sentiment analysis
  • Automated decision-making

Non-Functional Requirements

  • Explainability
  • Data privacy
  • Scalability

8. MVP Scope & Roadmap

Phase 1 (MVP)

  • Survey + ticket ingestion
  • Basic clustering

Phase 2

  • Trend detection
  • PRD export

Explicit Non-Goals

  • Fully autonomous insights
  • Customer-facing dashboards

9. Analytics & Measurement

Tracked Events

  • Feedback ingested
  • Cluster created
  • Insight reviewed
  • Action taken

Decision Triggers

  • Low action rate → Improve summaries
  • High false positives → Adjust models

10. Outcome & Learnings

  • Faster identification of systemic issues
  • More objective product prioritization
  • Reduced reactive firefighting

11. Key Learnings

  • AI is most valuable when augmenting PM judgment
  • Explainability drives adoption
